# Pandora's Box - User Study

## Experimental Design
- **Independent Variable**: 2 different types of explanations for the decision tree (decision rules vs. feature-importance scores)
- **Dependent Variable**: User trust
- **Null Hypothesis**: No measurable difference in user trust between decision rule-based explanations and feature-importance scores-based explanations
- **Significance Level**: 0.05
- Between-subject design (no specific types of participants needed, so numbers will likely not be an issue)

## Participants
- Laymen, preferably from diverse fields, ages, ethnic backgrounds, etc. (including people with disabilities, illiteracy, etc.)

## Materials
- Explanation of the scenario
- Questionnaire (can be filled in remotely)

## Questionnaire Design
- Control questions (ask the same question again later, just differently formulated)
- Objective questions (e.g. `according to this explanation, what is the most important feature?`)
- ~5 examples from the dataset, incl. explanations for A-B testing (Likert Scale)

## Analysis
- Drop non-serious responses (minimum time taken + control questions)
- Objective questions: Accuracy Rate
- Subjective questions: Mann-Whitney U Test

## Ethical Issues
- Participation is voluntary
- We need to collect *some* sensitive information (age, gender, country of origin, a socioeconomic indicator, special medical conditions) to confirm our findings aren't biased
- We will state clearly at the start of the study that this information will only be used for research purposes and will be deleted after
- First question: `Do you give permission for us to collect sensitive information for research purposes?`
- We will ask for permission to collect this information from the UM ethics committee, and data will be collected and stored on secure UM servers
